## RED test outline (Step 2)
`apps/worker/test/composition/real-provider-transport-gate.test.ts`:
1. **`gate_is_enabled_true_literal`** — Asserts: returned gate `.enabled === true`. Why: an assembled crossing bundle is armed by construction (the OFF path is "don't build it / leave `config.providerTransport` unset").
2. **`make_is_a_thunk_zero_invocation_at_build`** — Asserts: a `createRealProviderRunner` spy is called **0×** at `buildRealProviderTransportGate(...)`, and exactly **1×** when `gate.make()` is invoked. Why: L23/L27 factory-spy — building the bundle constructs nothing.
3. **`healthSource_rides_the_gate_not_config`** — Asserts: the returned object exposes `healthSource` on the **gate** (a `ProviderTransportGate`), and the helper produces no `config.healthSources` binding. Why: **Lesson-52** — the real source AND-locks to the transport flip only.
4. **`healthSource_omitted_fails_closed_to_unavailable`** — Asserts: `selectHealthSources(buildRealProviderTransportGate({...noHealthSource}), DEFAULT_HEALTH_SOURCES)` returns `UNAVAILABLE_HEALTH_SOURCES` (not the green stub). Why: L52 AND-lock — armed-without-source degrades closed.

`apps/worker/test/integration/boot-*.test.ts` (threading):
5. **`providerTransport_unset_is_byte_equivalent`** — Asserts: default `bootWorker` (no `providerTransport`) ⇒ the broker's runner is the stub (e.g. a completion is NOT routed to any injected transport; `backendsConfig` carries no `providerTransport` key). Why: shipped-default byte-equivalence.
6. **`providerTransport_armed_forwards_to_runner`** — Asserts: `bootWorker({ providerTransport: <armed gate over a fake HttpTransport> })` ⇒ the broker routes a completion through the fake transport (the `make()` runner is selected). Why: the drop-fix is the whole point; prove the gate reaches `selectProviderRunner`.
